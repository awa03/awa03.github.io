Journey To The Center Of The Earth

Interstellar space is a vacuum with a virtual absence of matter. The amount of matter increases approaching the sun. The sun ejects matter outwards into space. This is known as solar wind, it ejects protons (+ charge) and electrons (- charge). It streams outwards in all directions. The earth has a prominent dipolar magnetic feild which repels this solar wind. Van Allen belts is when the solar wind is deflected by the magnetosphere near the earth they are the strongest.

99% of the atmosphere is below 50km. The atmosphere is mostly nitrogen, infact oxygen was absent from the atmosphere before ~2.5 Ga.

Surface Feature

The surface is dominated by water and land. Water is part of the hydrosphere. Extremes of depth or height are rare. Most ocean floors are close to 5km depth.

Elemental Composition

Nintey percent of the earths atmosphere is comprised of:
- Iron (Fe) ~ 35%
- Oxygen (O) ~ 30%
- Silicon (Si) ~ 15%
- Magnesium (Mg) ~ 13%

Organic compounds are carbon containing compounds. Include wood, peat, lignite, coal, and oil. They are geologically rare. Metals are solids made of metallic elements. Melts are made of molten rock. Volatiles are materials that turn into gas at the surface. Minerals are inorganic crystalline solids. Glasses are non crystaline solides. Rocks are aggregate minerals. There are three types.

Igneous - Cooled from a melt
Sedimentary - Debris from pre-existing rock or minerals precipitated out of a water solution
Metamorphic - Rock altered by pressure and heat.

The Moho discontinuity seperates the crust from the upper mantle. It was discovered in 1909 by Andrija Mohorovicic. It is marked by change the change in velocity of seismic waves.

There are two types of crusts. The continental crust underlies the continents. The average rock density about 2.8g/cm. The average thickness is 35-40 km. It is granitic in composition. Oceanic crust underlies the ocean basins. There denisty is about 3.0 g/cm the average thickness is about 7-10 km. It is Basaltic in composition. Continental crust is less dense and floats higher. Oceanic crust floats lower and it is more dense. Oxygen is the most abundant element in the crust.

Earth's Mantle

It is a solid rock layer between the crust and the core. It is 2,885 km thick, and makes up 82% of the earths volume. It is composed of ultramafic rock (perdotite). Below ~ 100-150 km the rock is hot enough to flow. There are Three subdivisions: upper, transitional, and lower.

The Core

An iron-rich sphere with a radius of 3,471 km. The outer core is liquid iron-nickle-sulfur. Its density is ~ 10-12 g/cm. The inner core is solid iron-nickle alloy. Its density is ~ 13 g/cm

Lithosphere-Asthenosphere

The lithosphere is the outermost layer of the earth. It is 100 - 150 km of the earth. It behaves as a non-flowing, rigid material. It is made up of 2 components: crust and part of upper mantle. The asthenosphere is made up of the upper mantle below the lithosphere. It is shallower under oceanic lithosphere. It flows as a soft solid.
