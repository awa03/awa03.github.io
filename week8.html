In the example of Anna and Paul: Paul liebt Anna or Paul Loves Anna, Anna is the direct object and Paul is the subject of the sentence. Accusative for ask whom (wen) or what(was). This is known as der Wenfall (whom-case).

This causes the masculine articles "der" and "ein" to change when used in the accusative form. "Der" turns into "den" and "ein" into "einen". The feminine articles "die" and "einen" dont change. The same is true for "das" and "ein"(neuter articles).We need an –en and –en in the masculine accusative and an –en for the plural accusative weak declension. (Weak declensions do not indicate the gender/case of the noun because they have almost no variation (there are just two options for any gender/case combo: -e or -n).)

Accusative Transformations

der ---> den
ein ---> einen
die ---> die
eine ---> eine
das ---> das
ein ---> ein

Example

Die Frau den Mann. --- The women loves the man.

Only masculine articles change in the accusative

Paul mag den Rotwein. --- Paul likes the red wine.
Das Kind kauft einen Apfel. --- The child buys an apple.
Sarah trinkt einen Tee. --- Sarah is drinking a tea.
Sandra liest ein Buch. --- Sandra is reading a book.

Past tense class lecture

gehen -> gegangen
adding ge, and conj the second verb. Must memorize most of these.
Ich habe zu Mittag gegessen
Ich bin nachmittags zu Gause geblieben
Ich lesen das Buch -> Ich habe das Buch gelesen
Wir sehen den Mann -> Wir haben den Mann gesehen
Ihr geht im Park spazieren -> Ihr seid im Park spazieren gegangen

When talking about movement, a change in state, and to arrive we will use sein.
We will also use sein when our verb is sein, if not use haben.

When learning the verbs, learn them backwards and forwards. May need to double up on verbs in order to state in the past tense.
